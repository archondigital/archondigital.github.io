A Slideshow Experiment using Reveal.js by Archon Digital

Navigate by swiping or by using your arrow keys.

What is Reveal.js?

Reveal.js is a framework for developing presentations using HTML and was created by Hakim El Hattab.

Swipe downward or press the down arrow key.

I started playing around with reveal.js a few months ago while I was looking for ways to display a slide presentation inline on a post without having to embed PDFs or slideshows from an external source.

Swipe or press the right arrow key.

What can I use Reveal.js for?

  • Briefers
  • Slide Presentations
  • Infographics
  • other visualizations
Swipe or press the right arrow key.

A WordPress Post Template

For this experiment, I implemented the slideshow as a post template on WordPress on my blog. Each slide is generated from *section* elements that you hardcode on the HTML editor.

Swipe or press the right arrow key.

Responsive and Plays fairly well with Foundation Framework

I've been testing this on my iPhone and on an Android device and it works almost perfectly. In fact, I'm writing this post from an iPad Mini.

Swipe or press the right arrow key.

Where do I intend to use it?

The idea is to use this framework to create interactive infographics and slide presentations for briefers and Institutional Memory.

Swipe or press the right arrow key.

Update: I implemented this on 2013 Yearend Infographic/Slideshow of the Official Gazette which was published December 2013

Want to see more features?

I won't be showing any further features here as I plan to showcase the rest of Reveal's capabilities on actual infographics.

You can view a fully-functional demo showcasing all its features on the author's website.

Swipe or press the right arrow key.

Not into coding? No problem!

Not everyone wants to get their hands dirty with code but that doesn't mean you can't use this to wow your boss or something. Slid.es is a free visual online editor for creating mind-blowing Reveal.js slideshows.

Swipe or press the right arrow key.
